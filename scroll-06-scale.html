Zero Sum SVD

Balancing Loss Sensitivity for Low-Rank LLM Compression

Abstract

Advances in large language models have driven strong performance across many tasks, but their memory and compute costs still hinder deployment. We propose Zero-Sum SVD, a post-training method that performs global singular-component selection using activation whitening and first-order calibration-loss estimates.

Contributions

Three ideas.

01

First-order singular sensitivity

Each component scored by its directional derivative on the calibration loss.

02

Zero-sum global selection

Two-heap greedy rule keeps the running predicted loss change near zero.

03

Truncate–correct–re-truncate

One projected-gradient step recovers loss; re-truncation incurs negligible error.

Method · Scoring

Ranking singular values by their effect on the loss.

Let $W$ be a layer's weight and $X$ its calibration activations. The whitened gradient $H$ tells us how each direction of $W$ moves the loss. Combined with the SVD of the whitened weight, the first-order change from zeroing the $i$-th singular value is a signed sensitivity score.

Both magnitude and sign matter.
